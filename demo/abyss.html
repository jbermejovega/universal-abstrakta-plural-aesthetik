The Depth Zones

200–1,000m

Twilight Zone

Faint light still filters down here, just enough for bioluminescent signals to outshine the sun.

1,000–4,000m

Midnight Zone

Total darkness. Pressure builds fast, and life adapts with slow metabolisms and strange, glowing bodies.

4,000–6,000m

Abyssal Zone

Near-freezing water and crushing pressure define this vast, flat plain covering most of the seafloor.

6,000m+

Hadal Zone

Named after Hades. Confined to ocean trenches, this is the most extreme and least explored habitat on the planet.

Why the Deep Matters

  • Over 80% of the ocean remains unmapped and unexplored.
  • The deep sea stores more carbon than all forests on Earth combined.
  • Some deep-sea organisms have been alive for over 400 years.
  • Hydrothermal vents host ecosystems that run on chemistry, not sunlight.
