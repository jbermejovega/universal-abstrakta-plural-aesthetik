How hosting works

From application to your first jar of honey, here's the full timeline.

  1. Apply & site visit

    Submit the form below. A cooperative beekeeper visits within two weeks to confirm sun, water, and space requirements.

  2. Hive installation

    We deliver and install a starter colony, fully insured and labeled with cooperative contact information.

  3. Monthly inspections

    Our team visits monthly to check colony health. You're welcome to observe or learn hands-on.

  4. Harvest & share

    Each harvest, you receive jars of honey from your own hive plus an impact report on local pollination.

Frequently asked questions

Is it safe to host a hive near children or pets?

Yes. Honeybees are not aggressive away from the hive entrance, and we position hives away from high-traffic paths. We also provide a short safety orientation for every household.

What if I'm allergic to bee stings?

We don't place hives in homes with a known severe allergy to bee stings among residents. Pollinator garden grants are a safe way to support bees without a hive on-site.

How much space does a hive need?

A single hive needs about a 3x3 foot footprint with morning sun and a nearby water source. Rooftops, balconies, and garden corners all work.

Is there a cost to host a hive?

No upfront cost. Hosting is free in exchange for hive access during inspections and a share of the honey going to the cooperative.

Ready to bring bees to your block?

Tell us about your space and goals. A cooperative beekeeper will follow up within five business days to schedule a site visit.
